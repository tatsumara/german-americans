Hannah Arendt

Hannah Arendt was a Jewish German-American political theorist and publicist. She worked as a journalist and published several articles on political philosophy.

She experienced politics as something to be afraid of, because she experienced wars as well as revolutions. Therefore, her skeptical way of thinking and her activities were related to the concept of freedom.


“Under conditions of tyranny it is far easier to act than to think.”

Biography

  • born on October 14, 1906 as Johanna Arendt in Linden (today Hannover)
  • 1924-1928: studied philosophy, theology and classical philology in Marburg, Freiburg in Breisgau and Heidelberg.
  • 1933: After a brief detention by the Secret State Police (Gestapo), she escaped to Paris via Karlsbad and Geneva, where she worked as a social worker for various Jewish organizations.
  • Membership in the "World Zionist Organization" (until 1943)
  • 1948-1952: Director of the Jewish Cultural Reconstruction Organization for the salvation of Jewish cultural property.
  • 1949/50: In this capacity she traveled to Germany for the first time after the end of the Second World War
  • 1951: Arendt received American citizenship.
  • 1968: Elected Vice President of the Institute for Arts and Letters.
  • 1968: Elected Vice President of the Institute for Arts and Letters.
  • 1970: She published the study "Power and Violence"
  • died on December 4, 1975 in New York City
